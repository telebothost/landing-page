1. Introduction

Welcome to TeleBot Host ("we", "our", or "us"). This Cookies Policy explains how we use cookies and similar technologies when you visit our website or use our TBL bot hosting services. It describes what cookies are, which types we use (first-party and third-party), and how you can control their use.

By continuing to browse or use our services, you agree to the use of cookies as described in this policy, unless you disable them in your browser or via available tools.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work, improve user experience, and provide information to site owners.

Cookies set by us are called "first-party cookies". Cookies set by other companies (such as analytics or advertising partners) are called "third-party cookies". These may be used to deliver features like analytics, security, and personalized ads.

3. Why We Use Cookies

We use first-party and third-party cookies for several reasons, including:

  • To operate our website and dashboard securely and reliably
  • To keep you logged in and remember your preferences
  • To understand how visitors use our website and improve performance
  • To protect our platform from spam, abuse, and malicious traffic
  • To show relevant advertisements, including Google Ads, where applicable

Some cookies are essential for the basic functioning of the site. Others are optional and used for analytics, advertising, and customization.

4. Types of Cookies We Use

4.1 Essential (Strictly Necessary) Cookies

These cookies are required for our website and services to function properly. Without them, core features such as login, account security, and basic navigation will not work.

  • auth_session – Keeps you logged in during your browsing session.
  • auth_id – Identifies your authenticated session and helps secure your account.
  • cf_* (Cloudflare cookies) – Used by Cloudflare to identify trusted traffic, improve security, and prevent malicious activity.

4.2 Analytics and Performance Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors use our website so we can measure and improve performance, detect errors, and optimize content.

  • Google Analytics – Collects aggregated information about page views, time spent, traffic sources, and general usage patterns.
  • Cloudflare Analytics – Provides security and performance-related analytics on traffic to our site.
  • Internal stats cookies – May be used to track basic usage of our dashboard features.

4.3 Functionality Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization, such as remembering your preferences or helping to secure forms.

  • Preference cookies – Remember your settings (such as language or UI preferences).
  • Google reCAPTCHA – Helps protect forms from spam and abuse.

4.4 Advertising and Marketing Cookies

These cookies are used by us and our advertising partners (including Google Ads) to deliver more relevant ads and to measure advertising performance.

  • Google Ads / AdSense cookies – Used to deliver, measure, and improve ads shown on our site, including interest-based or contextual ads.
  • Third-party advertising partners – Other ad networks or partners we may work with can set cookies to track ad performance and prevent fraud.

These cookies may track your visits to this and other websites in order to show ads that are more relevant to your interests. You can opt out of personalized advertising as described below.

5. Third-Party Cookies and Services

We rely on several third-party services that may place cookies on your device when you visit our website or interact with our content.

5.1 Google Services

We use various Google services that may set cookies, including:

  • Google Analytics – For visitor analytics and website usage statistics.
  • Google Ads / AdSense – To display ads and measure ad performance and effectiveness.
  • Google Tag Manager – To help manage scripts and tags for analytics and marketing.
  • Google reCAPTCHA – To protect our forms and login areas from bots and abuse.

Google may use collected data in accordance with its own Privacy Policy and Terms. You can learn more about how Google uses information from sites that use its services by visiting their official documentation.

5.2 Cloudflare

We use Cloudflare to improve security, performance, and reliability. Cloudflare may set cookies such as __cf_bm or similar to distinguish between humans and bots and to help mitigate malicious traffic.

5.3 Other Third Parties

From time to time, we may integrate additional analytics, security, or advertising partners. These partners may use cookies or similar technologies in line with their own policies. Where required, we will update this Cookies Policy to reflect new integrations.

6. Cookie Duration

Cookies used on our website may be either session cookies or persistent cookies:

  • Session cookies – Temporary cookies that remain on your device only while your browser is open and are deleted when you close it.
  • Persistent cookies – Remain on your device for a set period of time or until you delete them through your browser settings.

The exact duration of each cookie depends on its purpose and the provider. You can view and manage cookie durations through your browser’s cookie tools.

7. Your Choices and Controls

You have several options to control or limit how cookies are used on your device:

  • Adjust your browser settings to block or delete cookies.
  • Use built-in browser tools to clear cookies and site data.
  • Use advertising opt-out tools to manage personalized ads.
  • Use any cookie consent or preference tools we provide on our website (if available).

Please note that disabling certain cookies (especially essential ones) may affect the functionality of our website, dashboard, or login systems.

7.1 Browser Controls

Most browsers allow you to refuse, accept, or delete cookies. The methods for doing so vary from browser to browser. You can usually find these settings in your browser’s “Options”, “Settings”, or “Privacy” menu.

7.2 Analytics Opt-Out

If you do not want Google Analytics to collect data about your visits, you can install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on, available at: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout

7.3 Advertising Preferences

You can manage Google’s personalized advertising settings by visiting your Google Ads settings page or by using industry-wide opt-out tools available in your region (such as “Your Online Choices” in the EU or similar services).
